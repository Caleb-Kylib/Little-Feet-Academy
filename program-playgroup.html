Early Years Education

Play Group Program

A gentle, joyful introduction to learning for children aged 2 to 4 years.

🧸

Learning Through Play

Our Playgroup is designed to be your child's home away from home. For many children, this is the first school experience, so we prioritize emotional safety, routine, and warm caregiver relationships.

At this stage, children learn best through play. Daily activities include storytelling, sensory exploration, music, movement, and guided free-play that develop language and coordination naturally.

We also work closely with parents to support communication, toilet routines, independence, and healthy social behavior so every child transitions confidently to pre-primary.

Program Highlights

  • Emotion & Socialization

    Learning to share, take turns, and communicate feelings with peers and caregivers.

  • Music & Movement

    Daily singing, dancing, and rhythmic activities to develop coordination and joy.

  • Sensory Art

    Finger painting, clay molding, and hands-on crafts to spark creativity and fine motor skills.

  • Healthy Habits

    Daily routines include hygiene, rest, toilet-learning support, and nutritious snack time.

Common Questions

Everything parents ask about our Playgroup program.

Not at all! We understand that children develop at different paces. Our caregivers work in partnership with parents to support the potty training process with a gentle, consistent approach.

Playgroup runs from 8:00 AM to 12:30 PM for a half-day session. However, we also offer a full-day daycare extension for working parents that runs until 5:00 PM, which includes a supervised afternoon nap and playtime.

Parents are advised to pack a healthy mid-morning snack, a change of clothes, diapers/wipes (if applicable), and a clearly labeled water bottle. Hot nutritious lunches are provided for those staying full-day.

We maintain a low ratio of 1 adult to every 5 children in playgroup. This ensures each child gets close supervision, responsive care, and the individual attention needed at this critical stage.
